GitHub Copilot Pauses New Sign-Ups

GitHub paused new sign-ups for Copilot Pro, Pro+, and Student plans on April 20, then froze self-serve Business signups two days later. Meanwhile, a data policy announced in March took effect April 24, defaulting individual users into model training unless they opt out. The combination hit hard: tighter usage limits, Opus models stripped from Pro, and a train-by-default data policy kicking in just as GitHub locked the door to new customers.

Anthropic's DMCA Cleanup Took Down Thousands of Repos

While cleaning up its Claude Code source leak from March, Anthropic's DMCA takedown requests accidentally removed thousands of unrelated GitHub repositories, which the company called a mistake. Anthropic also temporarily banned the creator of OpenClaw, a clean-room alternative built from the leaked code. The episode shows how messy the aftermath of an AI source code leak gets when legal tools are applied at scale and collateral damage hits the open source community.

3 of 10

Anthropic Launches Mythos via Project Glasswing

Anthropic released a preview of Mythos, calling it the most powerful model it has ever built, through a cybersecurity initiative called Project Glasswing with 12 partners including Apple, Amazon, Microsoft, and Google. The model identified thousands of zero-day vulnerabilities, many one to two decades old, while scanning first-party and open source software. Anthropic restricted broader access because the same capabilities that find bugs could be weaponized for offensive hacking.

4 of 10

Mythos Already Breached by Unauthorized Users

Just two weeks after its controlled launch, Bloomberg reported that an unauthorized group gained access to Mythos through a third-party vendor. Members of a Discord channel dedicated to finding unreleased AI models guessed the model's online location based on Anthropic's known URL patterns. Anthropic said it found no evidence of impact on its systems, but the breach undercuts the entire controlled-release security model that justified keeping Mythos from the public.

Claude Opus 4.7 Sets a New Coding Standard

Anthropic released Claude Opus 4.7 on April 16, delivering major gains in software engineering, especially on the hardest tasks. Vision resolution tripled to 3.75 megapixels, the model scores 70% on CursorBench versus 58% for Opus 4.6, and it introduces a new xhigh effort level for fine-grained reasoning control. GitHub confirmed a 13% coding benchmark lift over Opus 4.6, while Anthropic noted that Mythos Preview still outperforms it on alignment and cyber capabilities.

OpenAI Releases GPT-5.5 and Eyes a Super App

OpenAI launched GPT-5.5 on April 23, calling it the company's smartest model yet, with gains in agentic coding, scientific research, and drug discovery. Co-founder Greg Brockman framed it as a step toward a unified "super app" combining ChatGPT, Codex, and AI browser into one service for enterprise customers. Chief scientist Jakub Pachocki said the pace of improvement will only accelerate, noting the last two years were "surprisingly slow" compared to what is coming.

The AI Compute Arms Race Hits $45 Billion

In a single week, Anthropic secured a $5 billion investment from Amazon tied to $100 billion in future cloud spending, and Google committed up to $40 billion in cash and compute at a $350 billion valuation. Google Cloud will provide 5 gigawatts of TPU capacity over five years, on top of a prior Broadcom deal for 3.5 gigawatts. The deals reflect a brutal reality: training and running frontier models like Mythos demands infrastructure at power-plant scale, and every major cloud provider is racing to lock in supply.

Microsoft Offers Its First-Ever Voluntary Buyouts

Microsoft announced voluntary retirement buyouts for the first time in its 51-year history, with up to 7% of US employees (roughly 8,750 people) eligible. The eligibility formula adds years of service to age, with a threshold of 70. After laying off 9,000 employees last summer, this softer approach follows the same pattern: AI-driven productivity gains are reshaping workforce planning, and major tech companies are now quietly adjusting headcounts downward.

9 of 10

SpaceX Eyes $60 Billion Cursor Acquisition

SpaceX is working with Cursor, the AI coding startup, and has secured an option to buy it for $60 billion, according to TechCrunch. The deal would give Elon Musk a major foothold in the AI developer tools market, pairing Cursor's popular code editor with SpaceX's engineering infrastructure. It also signals that AI coding tools are now valued like platforms, not utilities, as the battle for developer mindshare intensifies between GitHub Copilot, Claude Code, and Cursor.

Anthropic Builds an Agent Marketplace and Claude Design

Anthropic launched Claude Design on April 17, a new product for creating polished visuals like prototypes, slides, and one-pagers, while its CPO left Figma's board over reports of a competing product. Days later, Anthropic revealed a test marketplace for agent-on-agent commerce in its San Francisco office, where Claude agents negotiate and transact with each other. Together these moves show Anthropic expanding aggressively beyond text and code into design tools and autonomous economic activity.
